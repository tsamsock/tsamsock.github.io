Laws Protecting Social Media

Under the current law in America, social media platforms are free to conduct their own user policies to restrict usersâ€™ access or ability to post content. One of the most common laws used to defend social media companies from legal repercussions is 'Section 230'. This section in the Communications Decency Act gives social media companies protections which allow them to choose what content they restrict, and how. This legally means social media platforms can't be sued for removing content that they themselves view as "inappropriate" effectively legalizing censorship.

The correct implementation of this law was intended to provide protection for the common good of users and not serve the platforms' own self interest. Examples such as defamation,fraud, obscenity, and threats are not protected by the First Amendment. These examples of speech should be the only excuse for censorship in Social Media.

Recently, a bill was proposed by a Texas state Senator, Bryan Hughes, that would allow users who have been banned to take legal actions against the social media platform. This bill would allow people who feel they have been wrongfully or unlawfully banned to sue the social media companies in order to be reinstated. Overall, social media platforms have too much power today and laws which limit their control on public opinion and beliefs need to be implemented.
